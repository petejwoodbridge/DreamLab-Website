About DreamLab

A new kind of
organisation
built for the future of work.

We aren't a traditional agency, consultancy, or studio. DreamLab is a living collective — agile, adaptive, and tuned to the signal of what's happening right now.

Our manifesto

The world doesn't work
the way it used to.
Neither do we.

Old organisations were built to optimise — to standardise, scale, and protect what they know. That worked when change was slow and direction was clear. It doesn't work anymore.

Technology is now rewriting itself every few months. AI, immersive media, spatial computing, realtime systems — these aren't tomorrow's problems. They're this morning's. And they reward organisations that can move, sense, and adapt in weeks, not years.

DreamLab is our answer to that. A network, not a hierarchy. A collective, not a roster. Assembled around every brief, tuned to every signal — and designed to stay useful no matter how fast the ground shifts.

The old organisation

Built for stability

  • Fixed departments and siloed expertise
  • Quarterly cycles and annual plans
  • Processes that predate the technology
  • Hierarchy protects the status quo
  • Knowledge is frozen in playbooks
DreamLab

Built for change

History & future

From day one
to day next.

DreamLab launched in June 2024 with a simple conviction — that the North West UK could host a world-class creative technology practice. Less than two years in, the collective has supported over 100 businesses, delivered 35+ projects, and channelled £2.7M of investment directly into regional innovation.

We're only just getting started. Scroll the timeline for what we've built so far — and what we're building next.

The future of work

The next decade is being
invented right now. We intend to be
everywhere it happens.

The winners of the next ten years won't be the biggest companies — they'll be the fastest to sense, integrate and deploy new capability. That's why DreamLab is built as a live network, not a fixed firm. Our job is to stay at the frontier — so our clients never have to wonder what's coming next.
